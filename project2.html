Cyclistic Bike-Share Case Study

How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

  • View Project
Michael Bruno

I analyzed 12 months of Chicagoâ€™s Cyclistic bike-share data (~5.5 million rides) to uncover differences in usage between annual members and casual riders. Using SQL Server for data cleaning and analysis and Tableau Public for visualization, I found that members dominate weekday commuting with shorter rides, while casual riders take longer leisure trips on weekends and during summer months. Electric bikes emerged as the most popular option across both groups. Based on these insights, I recommended targeted marketing campaigns to convert casual riders into members, focusing on commuting benefits, seasonal promotions, and electric bike perks.
